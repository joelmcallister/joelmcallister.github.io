Taylor series for `f(x, y)`

The definition of the Taylor series for `f(x, y)` is similar to its univariate equivalent `f(x, y) = S_f \ (x, y) = sum_(k=0)^(oo) sum_(k=0)^(oo) \ a_(kl) (x-x_0)^k \ (y-y_0)^l` where `f(x, y)` and `S_f \ (x, y)` and all their partial derivatives coincide at `(x_0, y_0)`. The coefficient `a_(kl)` is defined as follows `a_(kl) = 1/(k!l!) \ (del^(k+l)/(dx^kdy^l)[\ f(x_0, y_0)\ ])`  

Taylor polynomials for `f(x, y)`

In practice, we will not be using the above doubly infinite sums. Calculating their convergence criteria is prohibitively difficult. Instead we will be using Taylor polynomials, specifically those terms for which `k + l` is at most `2`. `color(black)(P_2(x, y) =) f(x_0, y_0) + ((del f)/(del x))_(x_0, y_0)(x-x_0) + ((del f)/(del y))_(x_0, y_0)(y-y_0)`   `+ 1/2 ((del^2 f)/(del^2 x))_(x_0, y_0)(x-x_0)^2 + ((del^2 f)/(del x del y))_(x_0, y_0)(x-x_0)(y-y_0) + 1/2 ((del^2 f)/(del^2 y))_(x_0, y_0)(y-y_0)^2` More concisely, we may write `P_2(x, y) = f(x_0, y_0) + \ \ \ \ \ \ bb m^T grad f(x_0, y_0)`   ` + 1/2 \ \ \ bb m^T\ H_f\ (x_0, y_0) \ bb m \ ,`   with `bb m = [(x-x_0), (y-y_0)]\ .`
