Periodic functions

A function `f` is `n`-periodic `<=>` `f(x + n) = f(x)` `AA x in RR`. `f(x)` is defined `AA x in [m, -m]` and `f` is `2m` periodic `=>` `f` is defined `AA x in RR`. Sinusoids are the quintessential example of a periodic function. In fact, `f(x) = sum_(n=1)^N (\ a_n cos(nx) + b_n sin(nx)\ )` `=>` `f` is `2pi`-periodic. for `N in NN`. (note that `n in NN` - intuitively, this property would not hold for non-integer coefficients of `x`)

Trigonometric series and Fourier series

A series of the form `1/2 a_0 + sum_(n=1)^(oo) (\ a_n cos(nx) + b_n sin(nx)\ )` is said to be a trigonometric series. `AA x` for which a trigonometric series converges, we may define a function `f(x) := \ \ 1/2 a_0 + sum_(n=1)^(oo) (\ a_n cos(nx) + b_n sin(nx)\ )` Such a trigonometric series is said to be the Fourier series (representation) of the function `f`. If a Fourier series converges some `x in RR`, then it also converges for `x+2pi k, \ \ k in ZZ` (proof).
