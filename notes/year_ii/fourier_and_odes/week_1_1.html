Eigenfunction Eigenvalue Problem

 Consider the following ODE `y^″ + lambda y = 0`, for some constant `lambda in RR`. An equivalence may be observed between this ODE and the eigenvalue equation from linear algebra `A bb x = lambda bb x`, where the vector `bb x` is replaced with the (scalar) function `y`, and the matrix `A` is replaced with the operator `-d^2/(dx^2)`. (N.B. recall that both matrix multiplication and differentiation are linear transformations) Thus, given certain boundary conditions, we may determine the eigenvalues and corresponding eigenfunctions (since `y` is a function, not a vector) for the given ODE. First though, we should determine the general solution to this equation, which can be split into three cases. 1) Let `lambda = -alpha^2` `alpha in RR \ \\ \ {0}` (`=> \ lambda < 0` ). Then the general solution becomes `y_(-)(x) = Acosh(alpha x) + Bsinh(alpha x)`, for `A`, `B` arbitrary constants. (N.B. we may restrict to `alpha > 0` since `-alpha` yields the same `lambda` value and the altered sign of `sinh` may be absorbed into the arbitrary constant) 2) Let `lambda = 0`. Then we have `(d^2y)/(dx^2) = 0` `=>` `y_0(x) = cx + d,` for arbitrary constants `c` and `d`. 3) Let `lambda = beta^2` `beta in RR \ \\ \ {0}` (`=> \ lambda > 0` ). Then the general solution becomes `y_(+)(x) = Ecos(beta x) + Fsin(beta x)`, for `E`, `F` arbitrary constants. (N.B. once again, we may restrict `beta` to the positive reals) The imposition of boundary conditions will fix the values of the arbitrary constants. N.B.
