Acceleration

Since `bb v = v bb T`, we may note the following about acceleration `bb a(t) = (d bbv)/(dt) = (dv)/(dt) bb T + v (d bb T)/(dt)`   `= (text(change of speed)) + (text(change of direction))` Also note that the tangent vector is always orthogonal to its derivative w.r.t. `t`. `d/(dt) (bb T * bb T) = 2 (bb T * (d bb T) / (dt)) = 0,`     since `|bb T| = 1`   `=> bb T _|_ (d bb T)/(dt)` If we parameterise our equation in terms of arc length `s`, the relationship between `bb a` and `bb T` becomes much simpler (since `v(s) = 1 \ \ \ AA s`). `bb a(s) = (d bb T)/(ds)`  

Curvature and unit normal vector

We can thus define `K(s) = |bb a(s)|`   `bb N(s) = (bb a(s)) / (|bb a(s)|) = (bb a(s)) / (K(s))` where `K` represents to the magnitude of `bb a` and `bb N` represents its direction. N.B. the above defintion is only applicable when working in a parameterisation w.r.t. the arc length `s`. `bb N` is commonly referred to as the unit normal vector, since `bb T` is orthogonal to its derivative w.r.t. `s`, and thus `bb a(s)` and `bb N(s)` are normal to the curve at point `P: (x, y)`. `bb N _|_ bb T`   `K` is known as as the curvature at point `P: (x, y)`. N.B. for a circle of radius `R`,     `bb N = -bb r/R`,   and so the unit normal vector `bb N` always points towards the center of the circle.  

Tangent and osculation

  `C'` is tangential to `C` at `P`       `<=>`       `bb r(s_0) = bb r'(s_0')`     and     `[(d bb r)/(ds)]_(s_0) = [(d bb r')/(ds')]_(s_0')`   `C'` osculates `C` at `P`       `<=>`       `C'` is tangential to `C` at `P`     and     `[(d^2 bb r)/(ds^2)]_(s_0) = [(d^2 bb r')/(ds'^2)]_(s_0')`   `<=>`       `C'` is tangential to `C` at `P`     and     `K(s_0) = K'(s_0')`     and     `bb N(s_0) = bb N'(s_0')`   (" same `bb N` and `K` at `P` ") N.B.   to "osculate" means to kiss.   The tangent to a curve is a line, whereas a curve is osculated by a circle, known as the "osculating circle". The curvature of `C` is inversely proportional to the radius of `D`, the circle which it osculates `K(s) = 1/(rho(s))`   N.B.   `rho` is known as the radius of curvature. The unit normal vector `bb N` points towards the center of `D`.
