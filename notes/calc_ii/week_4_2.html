Acceleration

Since `bb v = v bb T`, we may note the following about acceleration `bb a(t) = (d bbv)/(dt) = (dv)/(dt) bb T + v (d bb T)/(dt)`   `= (text(change of speed)) + (text(change of direction))` If we parameterise our equation in terms of arc length `s`, the relationship between `bb a` and `bb T` becomes much simpler (since `v(s) = 1 \ \ \ AA s`). `bb a(s) = (d bb T)/(ds)` Also note that the tangent vector is always orthogonal to its derivative w.r.t. `t`. `d/(dt) (bb T * bb T) = 2 (bb T * (d bb T) / (dt)) = 0,`     since `|bb T| = 1`   `=> bb T _|_ (d bb T)/(dt)`  

Curvature and unit normal vector

We can thus define `K(s) = |bb a(s)|`   `bb N(s) = (bb a(s)) / (|bb a(s)|) = (bb a(s)) / (K(s))` where `K` represents to the magnitude of `bb a` and `bb N` represents its direction. `bb N` is commonly referred to as the unit normal vector, since `bb T` is orthogonal to its derivative w.r.t. `s`, and thus `bb a(s)` and `bb N(s)` are normal to the curve at point `P: (x, y)`. `bb N _|_ bb T`  

Tangent and osculation

  `C'` is tangential to `C`       `<=>`       `bb r(s_0) = bb r'(s_0')`   and   `[(d bb r)/(ds)]_(s_0) = [(d bb r')/(ds')]_(s_0')`
