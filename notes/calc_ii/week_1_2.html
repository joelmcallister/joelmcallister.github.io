Power series

A geometric series is an example of a power series `S(x) = sum_(k=0)^(oo) x^k`, which converges to `1/(1+x)` The formula for `ln(1 + x)`, as derived on the previous page, is also an example of a power series `T(x) = -sum_(k=1)^(oo) (-x)^k/k`, which converges to `ln(1+x)` Power series can be written as follows `S(x) = sum_(k=0)^(oo) a_k \ x_k`. ... or more generally `S(x) = sum_(k=0)^(oo) a_k \ (x - x_0)^k`,   for constant `x_0` Each term `b_k` is the product of a coefficient `a_k` and the `k`th power of `x`. Therefore, the partial sum `S_n(x)` of a power series is a `n`th  (or `n-1`th) degree polynomial in `x`.
