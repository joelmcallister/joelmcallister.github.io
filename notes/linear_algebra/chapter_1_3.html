The cross product (vector product)

  `bb v xx bb w = ((a), (b), (c)) xx ((d), (e), (f)) = ((bf - ce), (cd - af), (ae - bd))`   `bb v xx bb w` is orthogonal to both `bb v` and `bb w`. `bb v xx bb w` is a normal vector of `Pi`   `<=>`   linearly independent vectors `bb v` and `bb w` are parallel to plane `Pi`  

Properties of the cross product

  `bb i xx bb j = bb k` `bb i xx bb k = - bb j` `bb j xx bb k = bb i`   `bb v xx bb w = - bb w xx bb v`   (non-commutitive) `(lambda bb v) xx bb w = lambda (bb v xx bb w)`   (scalar-vector associative law) `bb u xx (bb v + bb w) = (bb u xx bb v) + (bb u xx bb w)`   (distributive law) `bb a xx (bb b xx bb c) != (bb a xx bb b) xx bb c`   (vector triple products are non-associative) `bb a * (bb b xx bb c) = (bb a xx bb b) * bb c`   (scalar triple products are pseudo-associative; see below) `bb v xx bb w = bb 0`   `=>`   `bb v` and `bb w` are parallel Therefore,   `bb v xx bb v = bb 0`. One useful application of the cross product is finding the area of a parallelogram whose sides are specified by displacement vectors.  

Angle property

  `|bb v xx bb w| = |bb v| |bb w| \ sin theta` ,  where `theta` is the angle between `bb v` and `bb w`, `0 <= theta <= pi, \ sin theta >= 0`   ( this is derived from the fact that `|bb v xx bb w| ^ 2 + (bb v * bb w) ^ 2 = [ \ |bb v||bb w| \ ]^2` )  

Alternative equation of a line

In the same way the dot product was used to describe a plane, the cross product can be used to describe a line - except using a direction vector rather than a normal vector. `(bb v - bb a) xx bb d = bb 0` `bb v xx bb d = bb a xx bb d` Remember that the cross product returns `bb 0` when the two vectors are parallel, whereas the dot product returns `0` when the two vectors are orthogonal. So when describing a line, we use the cross product and a direction vector, and when describing a plane, we use the dot product and a normal vector.  

Box product (scalar triple product)

  The box product is defined as follows: `[bb a, bb b, bb c] = bb a * (bb b xx bb c)` Geometrically, this represents the volume of the parallelepiped spanned by the three vectors. The following properties are true of the box product:

Cyclic invariance - invariance under cyclic permutation

  `[bb a, bb b, bb c] = [bb b, bb c, bb a] = [bb c, bb a, bb b]`  

Negation when any two arguments swapped

  `[bb a, bb b, bb c] = - [bb b, bb a, bb c] = - [bb c, bb b, bb a] = - [bb a , bb c, bb b]`  

Vector triple product

  The following property is true of the vector triple product: `bb a xx (bb b xx bb c) = (bb a * bb c) \ bb b - (bb a * bb b) \ bb c`
