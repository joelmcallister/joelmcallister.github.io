Vectors in real 3D space

 

Properties

  `bb x + (bb y + bb z) = (bb x + bb y) + bb z`   (associativity of addition) `bb x + bb y = bb y + bb x`   (commutativity of addition) `bb x + bb 0 = bb 0 + bb x`   (existence of a zero w.r.t. addition) `bb x + (- bb x \ ) = bb 0`   (existence of an inverse) `lambda (mu bb x) = (lambda mu) bb x`   (associativity of vector-scalar multiplication) `1 bb x = bb x`   (existence of a one) `0 bb x = bb 0`   (existence of a zero w.r.t. multiplication) `(lambda + mu) bb x = lambda bb x + mu bb x`   (first distributive law) `lambda ( bb x + bb y ) = lambda bb x + lambda bb y`   (second distributive law)  

Length

For   `x_1, ..., x_n in bbb R`, `|bb v| = |(x_1), (...), (x_n)| = sqrt(x_1^2 + ... + x_n^2) = sqrt(bb v * bb v)`  

Angle between two vectors

  `cos theta = (v * w) / (|v||w|)`. `bb v` and `bb w` are orthogonal   `<=>`   `bb v * bb w = 0`   `<=>`   `theta = pi / 2 or bb v = bb 0 or bb w = bb 0`.

Cauchy-Schwartz Inequality:

Since `|cos theta| <= 1`, `| bb v * bb w | <= |bb v||bb w|`   (with equality iff `bb v` and `bb w` are parallel)  

Using cross product:

  `sin theta = |bb v xx bb w| / (|bb v| |bb w|)`,   for non-zero `|bb v|` and `|bb w|`   (see notes on cross product)  

Properties of dot product

  `bb i * bb i = bb j * bb j = bb k * bb k = bb 1` `bb i * bb j = bb i * bb k = bb j * bb k = bb 0`   `bb v * bb w = bb w * bb v` `AA` vectors `bb v, bb w`   (commutativity) `(lambda bb v) * bb w = lambda (bb v * bb w)` `AA` vectors `bb v, bb w` integers `lambda`   (scalar-vector associativity) `bb v * (bb u + bb w) = bb v * bb u + bb v * bb w` `AA` vectors `bb u, bb v, bb w`   (distributive law) `bb v * bb v = |bb v| ^ 2 >= 0` `AA` vectors `bb v`, with equality iff `bb v = bb 0`   N.B. You can compute the distance between a point and a line by simply computing the dot product of the two (`bb a xx bb v`) and solving the resulting equation.
