Rotation matrices

The matrix `[(cos theta, -sin theta), (sin theta, cos theta)]` is called a rotation matrix.   N.B.   rotation matrix multiplication is commutative,   has a determinant of `1`, and has no real eigenvalues   (though it does have complex ones).  

Reflection matrix

The matrix `[(cos 2delta, sin 2delta), (sin 2delta, -cos 2delta)]` is called a reflection matrix. where `delta` is the (anticlockwise) angle between the x-axis and the mirror line. An intuition for this formula is the fact that reflection in this mirror line is equivalent to reflection in the x-axis, followed by a rotation of angle `2delta`. N.B.   a reflection matrix has a determinant of `-1`, and always has `1`   and `-1` as eigenvalues.  

Characteristic polynomial of `P^(-1)AP`

The characteristic polynomial of `D = P^(-1)AP` is the same as the characteristic polynomial of `A`. `det(P^(-1)AP - lambda I) = det(A - lambda I)`
